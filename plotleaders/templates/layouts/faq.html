What is this about?
On this website, you will learn more about the way people view leadership figures: who comes to their mind? Which qualities do they see in those leaders? Who is seen as the most authoritarian leader? The most aspirational? The least benevolent? Use our interactive graph to find out!
How does it work?
You can use our app to generate perceptual maps of leadership figures:
  1. Pick one variable that will appear on horizontal axis.
  2. Pick a second variable that will appear on the vertical axis.
  3. Next, you can (but don't have to) pick a third variable to color-code the leaders.
  4. Finally, you can decide to include (or not) specific types of leadership figures: politicians, celebrities...
You can then zoom by drawing a rectangle on the portion of the graph you wish to have a closer look at, and double-click to reset the zoom level.
Show me an example!
The leaders in the left part (right part) of the graph are seen as the least (most) authoritarian.

The leaders in the lower part (upper part) of the graph are seen as the least (most) competent.

The leaders represented by blue dots (red dots) are seen as the most (least) trustworthy.
Where does the data come from?
Data was collected in two waves:
  1. A first group of respondents was asked to name their top three leadership figures.
  2. A second group of respondents was asked to rate these leaders on different psychological traits such as authority, benevolence, warmth, competence, etc...
So far, more 2500 persons have participated to the study, thank you to each of them! If you would like to help us as well, you can take the survey now in less than 5 minutes!"
