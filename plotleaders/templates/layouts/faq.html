Frequently Asked Questions

What is this?

On this website, you will learn more on the way Americans view leadership figures: who do they name when asked about leaders? Which qualities do they see in those leaders? Who is seen as the most authoritarian leader? The most aspirational? The least benevolent? Use our app to view the results!

How does it work?

You can use our app to generate perceptual maps of leaders:
  1. Pick one variable to view on the horizontal axis.
  2. Pick a second one to view on the vertical axis.
  3. Optionally, pick a third one to color-code the leaders.
  4. Finally, you can also decide whether you want to view all leaders on the map, only the real ones, or only the fictitious characters.
You can then zoom by drawing a rectangle on the portion of the graph you want to have a closer look at, and double-click to reset the zoom level.




In this example graph:
  • The leaders in the left part (right part) of the graph are the least (most) authoritarian.
  • The leaders in the lower part (upper part) of the graph are the least (most) competent.
  • The leaders represented by blue dots (red dots) are least (most) trustworthy.

What about the data?

We have collected the data presented here in two waves. First, we have asked one group of American citizens to name people and characters who they view as leaders. They could nominate both real and fictional persons, and obtained from them a list of leaders. We then asked a second group of respondents to rate those leaders on a certain number of psychological traits: authority, benevolence, warmth, competence, etc... So far, 2500 persons have helped us rate those leaders!

Who is behind this project?
